Methodology & Assumptions

How we produce solar feasibility estimates and the assumptions we use.

Methodology Version: 1.2
Last Updated: January 2026
Geographic Calibration: Santa Cruz County, CA

Inputs used

Each assessment combines the following data sources:

  • Property address: Used to determine roof orientation, local climate zone, and applicable utility rates
  • Roof photographs: Analyzed to extract roof pitch, identify shading from trees or structures, and estimate usable roof area
  • NREL PVWatts database: Historical solar irradiance data and weather patterns for the property location
  • Utility rate structures: Current rates and net metering policies from PG&E for Santa Cruz County
  • Regional cost data: Recent installation cost averages from Santa Cruz and Monterey Bay area installers

Outputs provided

Each report includes:

  • Annual production estimate: Expected kilowatt-hours generated per year based on roof characteristics and local climate
  • Savings range: Estimated annual electricity cost reduction, presented as a range to reflect uncertainty
  • Payback period: Years until cumulative savings offset installation cost, under current utility rates
  • Environmental impact: Estimated CO2 offset in pounds per year
  • Feasibility recommendation: Clear statement of whether solar appears economically viable for the property

Standard assumptions

Unless property-specific data suggests otherwise, we apply the following baseline assumptions:

Parameter Assumption Notes
System size Based on available roof area, typically 4-8 kW for residential Limited by usable south/west-facing area
Panel efficiency 19-21% (current standard panels) Conservative estimate using proven technology
System losses 14% total (inverter, wiring, soiling, temperature) Based on PVWatts default loss assumptions
Annual degradation 0.5% per year Industry standard for modern panels
System lifespan 25 years Typical manufacturer warranty period
Installation cost $2.80-$3.20 per watt before incentives Santa Cruz County average, 2024-2025
Federal tax credit 30% (current ITC) Subject to legislative changes
Utility rate escalation 3% annually Conservative estimate based on PG&E historical trends
Net metering Full retail credit (NEM 2.0 grandfathered or NEM 3.0) Depends on utility interconnection date

Analysis process

  1. Roof assessment: Computer vision analysis extracts pitch angle and identifies shading obstacles from submitted photographs
  2. Orientation determination: Address geocoding determines roof azimuth and confirms findings from image analysis
  3. Production modeling: Data inputs feed into NREL PVWatts model adjusted for property-specific factors
  4. Economic calculation: Production estimates combined with utility rates and installation costs to project savings
  5. Feasibility assessment: Results evaluated against minimum viability thresholds (payback period, production levels, cost-benefit ratio)

What could affect accuracy

Estimates may differ from actual outcomes due to:

  • Roof structural factors not visible in photographs (age, need for reinforcement)
  • Local permitting costs or requirements beyond typical ranges
  • Actual electricity usage patterns differing from assumptions
  • Changes in utility rate structures or net metering policies
  • Installation cost variations based on specific contractor and complexity
  • Weather patterns deviating from historical averages
  • Equipment performance varying from manufacturer specifications

Sources and standards

Production modeling based on:

  • NREL PVWatts Calculator (version 8)
  • National Solar Radiation Database (NSRDB)
  • PG&E published rate schedules and net metering tariffs

Cost data derived from:

  • California Solar & Storage Association installer surveys
  • EnergySage marketplace data for Santa Cruz County
  • Local installer quotes (anonymized)
